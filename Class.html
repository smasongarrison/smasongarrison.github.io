Introduction to Applied Statistics: Course Page

Overview

Introduction to Applied Statistics is a Vanderbilt Summer Academy (VSA) course designed for gifted adolescents students. It is scheduled for Summer Session I (June 12-17).

Media stories about ``the latest scientific findings’’ can range from the plausible to the silly. This class will help you learn to separate truth from fiction by introducing you to the basic statistical methods and tools used in psychological research. You will learn to use R, a free and powerful statistical software package, to conduct and interpret basic scientific statistical tests. You will begin to recognize the difference between proper and improper use of data, especially the distinction between correlation and causation (e.g., why margarine consumption does not actually lead to more divorces in the state of Maine). For the final project, you will formulate and answer your own scientific research question using these new tools and real psychology data.

Schedule

How To Read This Schedule:

Day 1 (June 13)

Learning Outcome: Describe data

Morning: mean, median mode, sd, distributions
Afternoon: Research Project Introduction (students pick data from topic list), Select Readings from How to Lie with Statistics

Day 2 (June 14)

Learning Outcome: Research Design, Selection Bias

Morning: Kinds of designs – experiments, quasi-experiments, observation. Causation, within this context
Afternoon: Research Project (students delve into selection methods of their study; and basic demographics), Select Readings from How to Lie with Statistics

Day 3 (June 15)

Learning Outcome: hypothesis testing, t-tests

Morning: null hypothesis testing; t-tests
Afternoon: Research Project (students delve into basic hypotheses testing), Select Readings from How to Lie with Statistics

Day 4 (June 16)

Learning Outcome: Correlation

Morning: correlation; third variables, causation etc
Afternoon: Research Project (students delve into examining relationships within their data), Select Readings from How to Lie with Statistics

Day 5 (June 17)

Learning Outcome: Presention

Morning: Research Project (prepare presentation), Present projects
